A health card, also known as a medical card or healthcare identification card, is a vital document that provides individuals with access to healthcare services. Typically issued by government agencies or private healthcare providers, it contains essential information such as the cardholder's name, date of birth, and unique identification number. The card may also include details about the individual's health insurance coverage, medical history, and contact information for healthcare providers. Health cards play a crucial role in streamlining patient care, facilitating insurance claims, and ensuring that individuals receive the necessary medical attention when needed, promoting overall well-being and timely healthcare access

Patient History

A person's patient history is a detailed record of their medical, social, and personal background, including past illnesses, surgeries, medications, and lifestyle factors. This information is essential for healthcare professionals to provide personalized care, make informed diagnoses, and create effective treatment plans.

Prescription

A prescription is a medical document provided by a healthcare professional, enabling individuals to obtain specific medications or treatments. It outlines essential information like the medication name, dosage, administration instructions, and the prescriber's signature, ensuring safe and regulated access to necessary healthcare interventions.
